Glossary

Rate

Rate defines the rate of compensation for an employee’s base salary. It’s typically displayed as either annual salary or hourly rate.

Base Salary

Base Salary is the compensation paid to your employees for performing the basic duties of their job. It doesn’t include bonuses, incentives, benefits, or allowances.

Total Cash Compensation (TCC)

Total Cash Compensation or TCC is the actual amount of cash compensation paid in a year. You can calculate TCC by adding up actual base salary, actual bonus, and actual short-term incentives paid.
TCC = Base Salary + Annual Bonus/Incentives.

Pay Percentiles

25th: The 25th percentile represents a value where 75% of the population pays above this number and 25% pays below this amount.
50th: The 50th percentile, or the median, represents the middle of a group of measurements. 50% or the population pays above this value and 50% pays below the value.
75th: The 75th percentile represents a value where 25% of the population pays above this number and 75% pays below this amount.

Typical FLSA Status

The Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) is a U.S. federal law that established minimum wage, overtime pay, recordkeeping, and child labor standards affecting full-time and part-time workers. Not all jobs are covered by FLSA. Some are considered “exempt” from FLSA, while those covered are commonly referred to as "non-exempt" jobs.
